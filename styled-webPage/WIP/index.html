About Us

We are building a multidisciplinary team, including academic researchers and community stakeholder to develop community-based partnership to address major social and economic issues in a holistic manner utilizing advanced Smart Safe & Connected (SS&C) technologies. We intend to: (a) identify the current critical and existing infrastructure issues affecting transportation and crime; (b) build a community-based partnership of stakeholders who share a common vision to improve our community; (c) Utilize advanced SS&C technologies to address community-wide transportation and crime issues. The planned community-oriented web portal will be deployed by leveraging state-of-the-art Web technologies, and will be open to the public. This web portal will enable research collaborators and community stakeholders to share information and visualize outcomes in real time.

Events

LSU researchers across campus are working on solving public safety and transportation challenges using Smart City methods. We invite you to join us to learn about these capabilities, and explore how we as a city and region can pursue shared Smart City initiatives.

We plan to build a multidisciplinary team, including not only academic researchers but also various community stakeholders, to develop a cross-sector partnership in order to create a research concept that will promote and employ S&CC technologies to tackle social and economic issues in a holistic manner. Particularly, we will (a) identify the current critical issues and existing infrastructure related with transportation and crime, (b) build a cross-sector partnership of many stakeholders who share a common vision, and (c) put the team together with a singular focus and use S&CC technologies to address our most dire challenges: transportation and crime.
